---
title: One-time setup
description: "Done once, not per order: add a payment method, set a spending authorization, and get your API key and webhook secret."
---

Before your first call, complete a short setup at the link Lane gives you. This
happens **once** — not per order.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a payment method">
    This is the card every order will be charged to. Card data goes directly to
    our payment vault and never touches your servers or ours in raw form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a spending authorization">
    A per-order cap and an optional monthly ceiling. Orders that would exceed
    your authorization are paused (see [`needs_input`](/api/needs-input)) rather
    than charged.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get your API key and webhook secret">
    The key authenticates every request. The secret lets you
    [verify webhook signatures](/api/webhooks#verifying-signatures).
  </Step>
</Steps>

After setup, every order is a single headless call. There is no per-order
approval step and nothing for an end user to tap or confirm.

<Warning>
  Your API key spends from one wallet (the payment method you enrolled). Treat it
  like a secret: anyone holding it can place orders against your authorization.
</Warning>

## Pilot checklist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Receive your credentials">
    Lane gives you an API key, a webhook secret, and the one-time setup link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete setup">
    Add the payment method and set your spending authorization.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stand up your webhook receiver">
    And [verify signatures](/api/webhooks#verifying-signatures).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Place a live test order">
    To a real address, from a container or test agent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Working session">
    Run real purchases together with the Lane team.
  </Step>
</Steps>
