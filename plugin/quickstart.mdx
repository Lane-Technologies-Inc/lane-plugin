title: Quickstart
description: "Make your first purchase: say what you want to buy, approve it with a passkey, and let Lane check out for you."
---

Once you have a passkey-enabled card ([setup](/plugin/setup)), buying is a
sentence.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Say what you want">
    Tell Claude what to buy, where, and a budget:

    > *"Buy a 6-person tent from REI under $250."*

    There's no menu and no "demo or production?" question — your request is the
    trigger. The agent confirms you're set up, then drafts the purchase.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the plan">
    The agent summarizes what it's about to buy in plain English — items,
    merchant, and a total spending ceiling. If anything's off, just say so and it
    re-drafts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve with a passkey">
    The approval appears in your open Lane wallet. Tap your passkey once to
    authorize up to the ceiling. This single tap authorizes the whole errand —
    even if it spans multiple merchants.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Lane checks out">
    Lane drives the real merchant checkout — finds the item, signs in if needed,
    pays, and confirms — across every merchant in the purchase. The agent shows
    a live status line and tells you the moment it needs something from you (a
    sign-in, a one-time code, a shipping choice).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Done">
    When it finishes, the agent tells you what was bought and the total. You
    never fetched or pasted a card.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## A multi-merchant errand

A single request can span merchants — it stays **one** approval:

> *"Get me camping gear under $100 — a tent and backpack from REI, and some KIND
> bars from Amazon."*

That's one intent with two mandates (REI, Amazon). You tap once; Lane checks out
at both. See [the model](/concepts/model) for why one errand is one intent.

## When Lane needs you mid-checkout

Some merchants require a sign-in, a one-time code, or a shipping selection. When
that happens, the agent posts a clear message about **what** it needs and
**where** to do it (your open Lane wallet), then waits. You complete it in the
wallet — using your *real* merchant credentials or the *real* code the merchant
sends you — and Lane resumes automatically.

<Warning>
  Lane checks out at the **real merchant in every environment** — there is no
  sandbox or test merchant, and no fake "code that works." If you're asked for a
  one-time code, it's a real one the merchant sent to you.
</Warning>

## Resuming an interrupted purchase

If a purchase got interrupted, you don't start over — just say so. The agent
checks your active intents (`lane-cli intents`) and resumes checkout against the
matching one rather than re-drafting. Intents are kept for up to 3 hours.
